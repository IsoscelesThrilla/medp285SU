Lightning Bolt is the energetic and aggresive love child of drummer Brian Chippendale and bassist Brian Gibson. Since forming in 1994 while both were attending the Rhode Island School of Design, the Brians have been creating and performing some of the most interesting and ear-busting music ever made. Throughout the band's now 26 year journey, both Chippendale and Gibson have concocted some very unique ways of creating the intense walls of sound they are famous for.

Brian Chippendale's drumming is something that can not be ignored easily, to say the least. With his immense stamina, raw power and speed, Chippendale is arguably unrivaled in delivering energy through a drumset. A unique aspect of his performances are the handmade masks that he wears. Not only are these masks something cool to look at, they also have a function. Chippendale fits a payphone microphone near the mouth of the mask, and through this he can produce distorted, almost unintelligible vocals. Because of his masks and his drumming being such a specatle to witness, it's safe to say that Chippendale is the unintentional star of the duo.

Brian Gibson may be the quiet one of the two, but with his bass he creates an insane amount of loud and interesing sound, and arguably gives Lightning Bolt its unique flavor. Over the decades of the band's life, Gibson has created a complicated system of pedals, amps, and other types of equipment to create sounds that don't even seem like they're coming from a bass guitar.
